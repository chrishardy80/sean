Sean Conway
Screen Writer

Biography

Sean Conway was born and raised on a council estate in Batley, a small mill town in West Yorkshire. He attended Batley Parish C of E Primary School, Batley Grammar School and Greenhead College Huddersfield, before going on to film school at Northumbria University, where he graduated with a 1st Class BA Honours Degree.

His graduation film Rocco Paris premiered at the London Film Festival 2005, has played at over forty festivals in over ten countries and won many awards (including ITV Best Script to Screen Award, Imagine Award for Best Editing and a Commendation from the Royal Television Society).

Whilst still at University he sold his first feature film screenplay, Love, to the fashion photographer and founder of Dazed & Confused magazine Rankin (Lives of the Saints).

Sean then wrote and directed the short film Rabbit Stories, a Third Films production, which premiered at the London Film Festival 2006, has played at over seventy festivals in over twenty countries and has also been very successful (including getting a Special Mention of the Jury at Clermont-Ferrand, and being a finalist of both the Myspace Movie Mashup and the BBC New Filmmakers Award).

In spring 2007 he was picked to write and direct a Cinema Extreme film. Funded by Film Four and the UK Film Council, Cinema Extreme is the most prestigious short film scheme in the UK. Alex and her Arse Truck, a Third Films production, made its world premiere spring 2008 in Hamburg having screened at the London Film Festival 2007 and has already screened at sixty festivals across the globe. Alex and her Arse Truck will broadcast on Channel 4 summer 2008.

Rocco Paris, Rabbit Stories and Alex and her Arse Truck are all distributed by world sales agent Future Shorts. Sean has recently completed Kings of London, an IWC production which he wrote and directed as part of the Coming Up scheme, which premiered at the National Film Theatre in February 2008 and broadcasts on Channel 4 summer 2008.

He has directed two music video promos, A Place that Glows for Parisman and Floats around My Head for Reset for Saturn and has written a script entitled Ghosts & Lunatics for a multi-media collaboration with animator Stephen Agnew and musician Justin Lockey. He is currently working on his own stills photography and video installation exhibition entitled Shouting in their Sleep.

Sean has had many poems and short stories published in various publications, notably Poems for the Retired Nihilist and Full Moon Empty Sportsbag. Summer 2008 sees the release of his debut novella Son of Steve (published by Six Gallery Press) and he is currently writing his first novel The Science of the Beautiful.

He has been commissioned to write a feature screenplay entitled F*ckart for award winning director Ashley Horner (The Other Possibility) and the film will go into production summer 2008.

Sean has penned two feature screenplays that he will direct himself, the first being Young (Third Films, s16mm drama, 90’) and the other being Dog Knife Wife (Pinball Films, HD drama, 90’), both are currently in development, seeking co-production and funding.

Sean is writing other feature film screenplays Blood is Blood, Roberto Banana and Come See What Hunger Makes. He was also one of four filmmakers chosen to take part in the Film 4 & Director’s Guild Direct Access scheme and spent spring 2008 observing director David Yates on the set of Harry Potter and the Half Blood Prince.

Sean is part of the highly regarded Third Films stable. Third Films is a boutique film production company set up by producer Samm Haillay and writer director Duane Hopkins (Better Things). And much is expected of Conway’s collaborations with award winning cinematographer Lol Crawley (Ballast).

Ray Donovan
Writer/Producer
2013-

Ray Donovan is set in Los Angeles, California, where Irish-American Ray Donovan (Liev Schreiber), originally from South Boston, works for the powerful law firm Goldman & Drexler, representing the rich and famous. Donovan is a "fixer": a person who arranges bribes, payoffs, threats, and other shady activities, to ensure the outcome desired by the client. Good at his job, and no common hood, Ray is normally a devoted family man as well. He experiences his own problems when his menacing father, Mickey Donovan (Jon Voight), is unexpectedly released from prison, and the FBI attempts to bring down Ray and his associates.

All I See Is You is a 2016 psychological romantic drama film directed by Marc Forster and written by Sean Conway. The film stars Blake Lively and Jason Clarke. It was screened in the Special Presentations section at the 2016 Toronto International Film Festival.[3] The film was released by Open Road Films (the company's final film) in around 250 theaters in the United States on October 27, 2017.

Shameless
Writer
2013

Shameless is a British comedy-drama series set in Manchester on the fictional Chatsworth council estate. Produced by Company Pictures for Channel 4, the series aired from 13 January 2004 to 28 May 2013. The comedy drama, centred on British working class culture, was accorded critical acclaim by various sections of the British media, including The Sun newspaper and Newsnight Review on BBC Two. The programme was created and partially written by Paul Abbott, who is also the programme's executive producer. In 2005, the show won "Best Drama Series" at the BAFTA TV Awards and "Best TV Comedy Drama" at the British Comedy Awards. The network Showtime adapted the series into its own American version, which debuted in 2011.

Hit & Miss
Writer
2012

Hit & Miss is a British television series, created by Paul Abbott, broadcast on Sky Atlantic. It stars Chloë Sevigny as a transgender contract killer who discovers she has a child with her former lover. She struggles between caring for her newfound family and maintaining her job as an assassin. The show was conceived by Abbott as a combination of two ideas for separate TV series. All six episodes were scripted by Sean Conway. The series is Sky Atlantic's first original drama commission. It premiered on 22 May 2012, in the United Kingdom. In the United States, it premiered on DirecTV's Audience Network on 11 July 2012.
